# Task M5.5 — Convergence quality (hub-dampening) + re-run

**Status: M5.5 — IN REVIEW** (implemented on `feat/m5.5-convergence-quality`; awaiting review gate)

An evidence-driven slice inserted **after M6's real-data smoke**, which proved the convergence
signal was broken in practice. See [`docs/m6-smoke-notes.md`](../docs/m6-smoke-notes.md) and
PHASE_1_PLAN §7 (the convergence-quality risk).

## Why

The M6 smoke showed `/horizon` ranked **ubiquitous hub entities** ('GitHub', a prolific author) at
the top — it rewarded "loud everywhere," the exact opposite of "quietly emerging." The naive signal
counted raw distinct sources, so an entity mentioned everywhere trivially "converged." It also
showed a card's `why` ("convergence: 'X' across N sources") could disagree with its listed
`contributing_sources`.

## Goal

Make the convergence signal surface **rare-but-cross-source** entities (genuine weak signals),
reconcile the evidence the endpoint shows, and **re-validate on real data**. The canonical priority
class (`priority.py`) is untouched; hype still only demotes.

## Scope

- **Hub-dampening** in `app/scoring/graph_signals.py` (transparent + documented):
  - **Independence gate** — require ≥ `GRAPH_MIN_SOURCES` (default 2) *distinct* sources; the
    degree/centrality term is gated the same way (a single-feed author hub can't dominate).
  - **IDF weighting** — `idf = ln(total_developments / developments_mentioning_entity)`; ubiquitous
    hubs suppressed, rare entities amplified. Convergence ∝ `distinct_sources · idf`.
  - **Singleton suppression** — an entity confined to a single development contributes ~0.
- **Reconcile the evidence** — a card's `contributing_sources` is now the **driving entity's**
  distinct sources, so the `why` claim and the listed sources agree.
- **Re-run** on a larger independent feed set, exercising real semantic dedup
  (`embedded > 0`), and record before/after `/horizon` results.

## Non-goals

- No `priority.py` change, no graph-schema change, no new sources/fetchers, no M7 digest/feedback,
  no fuzzy entity resolution, no MCP/post generator.

## Acceptance criteria

- A ubiquitous hub (high raw sources, low IDF) ranks **below** a rare entity converging across ≥ 2
  independent sources; a single-source prolific entity does not dominate; a singleton scores ~0; the
  `why` evidence equals the driving entity's distinct sources.
- The priority class is unchanged and hype still demotes (`/horizon` still excludes
  operational/immediate).
- Re-run notes recorded in [`docs/m5.5-rerun-notes.md`](../docs/m5.5-rerun-notes.md), with real
  before/after `/horizon` evidence and `embedded > 0` proving semantic dedup ran.
